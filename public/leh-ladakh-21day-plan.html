Major festivals in window

🎭 Festival Calendar — Your Window

Arrive late July to catch the Phyang Tsedup Festival (Jul 22–23) at Phyang Monastery — one of Ladakh's most spectacular masked dance festivals with the legendary giant Thangka display. Then stay through late August for shoulder calm, with the grand Ladakh Festival anchoring September 21–24 if you extend. The Korzok Gustor at Tso Moriri (Jul 27–28) also falls within your opening week if you time arrival well.

🗓 Recommended Arrival: July 22–25, 2026 Arriving July 22–23 puts you at the Phyang Tsedup Festival on day 1–2. You'll have all of July (dry, sunny, peak season) and August (slightly cooler, equally clear) for your 21 days. Weekend treks are fully operational, monasteries are buzzing with life. If you can stretch to 5 weeks, the Ladakh Festival (Sep 21–24) is unmissable.
☀️ July–August Weather Daytime 20–28°C in Leh town. Nights drop to 8–12°C — keep a fleece handy. High passes (Khardung La, Chang La) can be 0°C even in August. Skies are mostly clear; occasional afternoon cloudbursts in August. Sunscreen is non-negotiable at this altitude.
🌐 Connectivity Reality Leh town now has reliable 4G (Airtel/BSNL) and many hotels have fiber WiFi. The Ladakh Coworking Hub and Coffee Culture Leh are your dedicated work spots. Outside Leh, connectivity drops sharply — plan offline work for weekend trip days.
⚠️ Altitude Acclimatisation — Non-Negotiable Leh sits at 3,524m. If flying in, do NOT do anything strenuous on Days 1–2. Rest, drink 3L+ of water daily, eat light. Symptoms of AMS (headache, nausea, breathlessness) are common. Days 1–2 in this plan are intentionally slow. Diamox (acetazolamide) is recommended — consult your doctor before travel.

The 21-Day
Blueprint

Daily itinerary · Work + Explore · Festival Timing

Week One — Arrival & Acclimatisation (Days 1–7)
01Sat

🛬 Arrive Leh — Absolute Rest Day

Fly into Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport (ideally morning flight for best views). Transfer to accommodation. DO NOT exert yourself. Unpack slowly. Drink 3L water. Short stroll to Main Market in the evening only if you feel fine. No alcohol for 48 hours. Check in at Phyang Monastery info for the next day's festival.

Dinner: Room service or gentle walk to Bon Appetit or Amdo Food near Changspa
02Sun

🎭 Phyang Tsedup Festival — Day 1

Phyang Tsedup Festival (Jul 22–23) — Hire a local taxi to Phyang Monastery (17km from Leh). Witness the Cham masked dances and the dramatic unfurling of the giant Thangka. This is one of Ladakh's most visually spectacular festivals. Return to Leh by afternoon. Rest.

Lunch: Festival stalls at Phyang · Dinner: Tibetan Kitchen, Leh
03Mon

🖥 Work Day + Old Town Orientation

First proper work morning from your hotel WiFi or Ladakh Coworking Hub (Fort Road). Set up workflow. 9am–2pm. Afternoon: walk Old Town, Soma Gompa, Main Bazaar. Evening at Leh Palace viewpoint for sunset — free, extraordinary.

Breakfast: Hotel · Lunch: Namza Café, Changspa · Dinner: Open Sky Restaurant
04Tue

🖥 Deep Work Day + Shanti Stupa

Full morning work session. Afternoon walk to Shanti Stupa (short hike, 20 min from Changspa) — incredible 360° views of Leh Valley. Early evening: explore Changspa Road's café strip.

Café Work: Coffee Culture Leh · Dinner: Bon Appetit Restaurant
05Wed

🖥 Work + Leh Palace & Hall of Fame

Work morning. Afternoon visit Leh Palace (₹25 entry, 400-year-old Namgyal dynasty palace). Then Hall of Fame museum — excellent for understanding Ladakh's military and cultural history. Evening: Main Market shopping for local crafts and pashmina.

Lunch: Leh Town Café (Fort Road) · Dinner: Gesmo Restaurant (classic)
06Thu

🖥 Work + Indus Valley Drive

Work until 1pm. Afternoon half-day taxi to Thiksey Monastery (19km) and Hemis Monastery (45km) — two of Ladakh's most iconic gompas. Thiksey at sunset is stunning. Book taxi for ~₹1,800 for the afternoon run.

Lunch: Spitok village dhaba · Dinner: Chopsticks Restaurant, Leh
07Fri

🖥 Work + Prep Weekend Trek

Work morning. Book your Weekend 1 trek (Sham Valley or Stok) with your chosen agency by noon — confirm pickup time. Afternoon: pack gear, get permits if needed, stock up on water and snacks from Main Market. Early dinner and early sleep.

Dinner: The Tibetan Kitchen · Early night by 9pm
Week Two — Finding Flow (Days 8–14)
08Sat

🥾 Weekend Trek 1 — Sham Valley / Stok Day Hike

See Treks section for full details. Either a guided full-day hike in Stok Valley (monastery + ridge views) or the first day of the Sham Valley 2-day circuit with homestay overnight.

Packed lunch from agency · Dinner: Homestay or back in Leh
09Sun

🥾 Trek Day 2 or Recovery + Pangong Planning

Complete Sham Valley Day 2 or rest and recover in Leh. Begin planning Pangong Tso weekend trip — book taxi through your hotel or a reputable driver (₹5,000–6,500 one way). Note: Inner Line Permit required for Pangong (₹400, get via district magistrate or agency).

Brunch: Bob's Café · Dinner: Julelay Restaurant
10Mon

🖥 Productivity Day + Local Market Deep Dive

Strong work day. Set up your weekly schedule template. Afternoon: explore the Craft Bazaar behind Main Market — Tibetan jewellery, thangka paintings, Ladakhi rugs. Evening: Lehvenda Café for a long sunset coffee session.

Lunch: Neha Snacks, Main Bazaar (street food) · Dinner: Hotel dining
11Tue

🖥 Work + Stok Palace Museum

Morning work. Afternoon half-day trip to Stok Village (15km south of Leh) — visit the Stok Palace Museum with royal family artefacts and thangkas. The Stok valley walk is also a gentle 2km trail through apricot orchards.

Café work: Wanderer's Terrace Café · Dinner: Open Sky Café
12Wed

🖥 Work + Sangam Point

Work morning. Afternoon to Sangam — the confluence of the Indus and Zanskar rivers (35km). This is one of the most visually dramatic spots near Leh and a great 3-hour afternoon excursion. Taxi ~₹1,500 shared or ₹2,500 private.

Lunch: Roadside dhaba at Sangam · Dinner: Little Italy, Leh
13Thu

🖥 Work + Diskit / Nubra Planning

Work until 2pm. Book Weekend 2 transport to Nubra Valley (₹5,500–7,000 for 2-day taxi via Khardung La). Apply for Inner Line Permit for Nubra Valley (can do same day at DC office or online). Afternoon rest. Pack for the weekend.

Dinner: Tibetan Kitchen + early sleep
14Fri

🖥 Work + Alchi Day Trip

Half day work. Afternoon trip to Alchi Monastery (70km west of Leh) — 11th-century Tibetan Buddhist murals, completely unique art style, one of Ladakh's cultural crown jewels. One of Ladakh's best half-day excursions. Taxi ~₹3,500.

Lunch: Alchi Kitchen Restaurant (vegetarian, excellent) · Dinner: Back in Leh
Week Three — Deep Ladakh (Days 15–21)
15Sat

🌄 Weekend 2 — Nubra Valley via Khardung La

Early start (6am) to cross Khardung La (5,359m — world's highest motor-able road). Descend into Nubra Valley. Visit Diskit Monastery and the 32m Maitreya Buddha statue. Afternoon: Hunder sand dunes and Bactrian double-humped camel ride. Overnight at a Nubra Valley campsite or guesthouse.

Packed breakfast · Lunch: Sumur village café · Dinner: Campsite
16Sun

🌄 Nubra Valley Day 2 — Turtuk Village

Drive to Turtuk (95km from Hunder) — the last accessible village before Pakistan's border. Beautiful apricot and mulberry orchards, Balti culture, narrow lanes. Return to Leh in the evening via Khardung La.

Lunch: Local Balti food in Turtuk · Dinner: Recovery meal in Leh
17Mon

🖥 Recovery Work Day + Market Evening

Rest morning + lighter work schedule. Afternoon and evening at leisure in Main Market. Stock up on local apricot products, saffron, dried fruit. Evening coffee at Leh Town Café on Fort Road.

All meals: Light, in-town, no heavy planning today
18Tue

🖥 Deep Work Day + Magnetic Hill

Strong work morning. Afternoon drive to Magnetic Hill (30km) — the optical illusion where vehicles appear to roll uphill. Also visit Gurudwara Pathar Sahib (5km further) and Likir Monastery on the return. ₹2,000–2,500 shared taxi circuit.

Lunch: Gurudwara langar (free) · Dinner: Bon Appetit
19Wed

🖥 Work + Lamayuru Planning

Work day. Book Weekend 3 trip to Pangong Tso (permits ready from Day 9). Plan departure for Saturday 5am. Pack camping gear or confirm lakeside campsite booking (dozens of options around ₹1,500–3,000/night).

Dinner: Splurge at The Grand Dragon restaurant
20Thu

🖥 Work + Farewell Market Walk

Final full work day. Afternoon: final Main Market shopping — pashmina shawls, Ladakhi jewellery, thangka art, yak wool blankets. Order a custom handicraft piece if you want a unique memento. Last sunset at Shanti Stupa.

Dinner: Long dinner at Chopsticks — your favourite by now
21Fri

🌅 Weekend 3 Begins — Pangong Tso Departure

Pre-dawn start (5am) for Pangong Tso via Chang La (5,360m). Arrive by 11am at the iconic blue-green lake. Camp at the water's edge. This is the crown jewel of Ladakh — 3 days of colour-shifting water, starlit skies, and total silence.

Packed breakfast · Lunch + Dinner: Pangong campsite
🏕 Pangong Extension If you can squeeze 3 nights here (Days 21–23), Pangong is worth it. The lake colours change from turquoise to cobalt to silver depending on cloud and time of day. Sunrises here are among the most extraordinary experiences in India. Add Spangmik and Merak villages for a fuller circuit.

Weekend Treks
& Hikes

3 Weekends · Curated Routes · Company Recommendations

🥾 Trekking Season Note July–August is peak trekking season in Ladakh. Passes are clear, weather is stable (mornings especially), wildflowers are in bloom in river valleys. You do not need technical mountaineering skills for the treks listed — just solid fitness, acclimatisation, and good boots.
01

Sham Valley Trek (Village Circuit)

Weekend 1 · 1–2 Days · Difficulty: Easy-Medium · Max Alt: 4,100m

The gentlest introduction to Ladakhi trekking. Passes through ancient villages of Yangthang, Hemis Shukpachan, and Temisgam — traditional Ladakhi stone houses, apricot orchards, monasteries perched on cliffs. Can be done 1-day (12km) or 2-day with a homestay overnight.

Highlights: Ribbok monastery, yak and dzos grazing on high meadows, local butter tea in a village home, traditional Ladakhi architecture unchanged for centuries.

Beginner Friendly Village Homestay No Permits
02

Stok Kangri Base Camp Day Trek

Any Weekend · 1 Day · Difficulty: Medium · Max Alt: 4,800m

The most popular day hike from Leh. From Stok village (15km from Leh), hike up through the Stok Valley past grazing meadows and glacial streams to Stok Kangri's base camp with jaw-dropping views of the Karakoram and Zanskar ranges.

Distance: 12km round trip from Stok village · Duration: 7–8 hours

Best Day Hike 360° Views Altitude Gain
03

Markha Valley Trek (Section)

Weekend 2 Alternative · 2 Days · Difficulty: Medium-Hard · Max Alt: 5,150m

The most famous multi-day trek in Ladakh. You can do a 2-day section (Skiu to Markha or Markha to Nimaling) for a weekend. Cross the Markha River multiple times, pass through remote villages, camp under impossible stars. 4WD jeep from Leh to Skiu trailhead (40km, ~₹3,000).

Highlights: Kang Yatse peak views, Markha village gompa, yak herders at Nimaling high camp, incredible solitude.

Getting Around
Ladakh

Taxis · Bikes · Permits · Rules You Must Know

⚠️ No Self-Drive Car Rentals in Leh Since 2015, the Ladakh Taxi Union bans all outside-registered rental cars from sightseeing within Ladakh. This means no Zoomcar/Revv/self-drive within the region. Your options are: (1) hire a local Leh-registered taxi driver, (2) rent a motorcycle in Leh for local sightseeing, or (3) use shared jeep services for popular routes. This is actually fine — local drivers know roads, passes, and hidden spots far better than any GPS.
🚗 Best Transport Setup for 21 Days Establish a relationship with ONE reliable local driver from Day 1. They'll give you better rates for repeat bookings, priority availability, and genuine local knowledge. Ask your hotel to recommend one. Alternatively, the Leh Taxi Union stand near the Main Market has fixed-rate taxis for all destinations.

Bike Rentals in Leh
🏍 Motorcycle Rentals Royal Enfield Himalayan or Bullet 500cc — most popular choice. Cost: ₹1,200–2,000/day. Multiple rental shops on Fort Road and Changspa Road. Recommended for experienced riders only — mountain roads demand respect. Carry all permits and ID. Fuel up before leaving Leh (petrol stations sparse).

Recommended Shops: Enfield House (Fort Road) · Himalayan Bike Rentals (Changspa) · Always inspect the bike thoroughly before taking it.
Getting to Leh
✈️ By Air (Recommended) Direct flights from Delhi (~1hr), Mumbai (~2hr), Chandigarh, Jammu. IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet all operate routes. Book 6–8 weeks ahead for July — peak season, seats sell out fast. Morning flights offer stunning Himalayan views.
🚗 By Road (Adventure Option) Manali–Leh Highway (~47 hours, open June–October) or Srinagar–Leh Highway (2 days with overnight in Kargil). Epic road trip but not practical for a solo workation arrival. Consider for your return if you have flexibility.
Inner Line Permits
📋 Permits Required You need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) for: Pangong Tso, Nubra Valley, Tso Moriri, Hanle. Cost: ₹400–600 per person per destination. Get them at the District Commissioner's Office in Leh (near Main Market, takes ~1 hour) or through your taxi driver / trekking agency (most will arrange for a small fee). Bring 2 passport-size photos and a copy of your ID. Also required: Restricted Area Permit (included with ILP) for some zones.

Must-Do
Experiences

Beyond the usual tourist circuit

Culture · Free

Leh Palace at Dawn

Arrive at Leh Palace (Leh Khar) just as it opens at sunrise. You'll have the 17th-century Namgyal royal palace almost to yourself for 30 minutes. The views of the Zanskar range and Leh town are extraordinary in early morning light.

Entry: ₹25 · Best time: 7–8:30am · Open daily
Spiritual · Unique

Monastery Morning Prayers

Ask any monastery if you can attend the early morning prayer ceremony (puja). Thiksey and Hemis monasteries allow respectful visitors. The sound of drums, horns, and chanting at 6am in a 600-year-old monastery is unforgettable.

Best at: Thiksey (arrive by 6am) · Donation-based
Adventure · Thrilling

Zanskar River Rafting

The Zanskar River near Nimmu (45km from Leh) offers Grade III–IV white water rafting. Half-day trip, runs July–September when water levels are right. Multiple agencies offer this — ₹1,500–2,500 per person.

Duration: 3–4 hours on water · Season: July–Sept
Astronomy · Mind-Bending

Stargazing at Shanti Stupa

Ladakh has some of the darkest skies in India. Walk up to Shanti Stupa after 10pm on a clear night. At 3,600m+ with zero light pollution, you'll see the Milky Way as a solid river of light. The Ladakh Festival also has an astronomy program at Shanti Stupa.

Best nights: New moon · Take a warm jacket
Local Life · Authentic

Village Homestay Dinner

Book a homestay dinner in Stok or Alchi village — a Ladakhi family cooks a traditional meal (skyu, thukpa, tsampa, local apricot brandy). This single experience often becomes the highlight of people's entire Ladakh trip.

Arrange via: Your trekking agency or hotel · ₹400–700/person
Offbeat · Remote

Lamayuru Moonscape

The village of Lamayuru (125km west of Leh) sits in a surreal eroded lunar landscape — otherworldly clay formations, one of Ladakh's oldest monasteries, complete isolation. A full-day excursion by taxi (₹4,000–4,500).

Combine with: Alchi and Likir on same route
Shopping · Cultural

Ladakhi Craft Bazaar

The lanes behind Main Market hide excellent craft shops: handwoven pashmina, silver jewellery set with turquoise and coral, Tibetan singing bowls, thangka paintings, Ladakhi wool rugs. Buy directly from weavers where possible.

Best street: Behind Jama Masjid, Old Town
Festivals · Spectacular

Phyang Tsedup (Jul 22–23)

If you time your arrival right, this two-day festival at Phyang Monastery features sacred Cham masked dances by monks in elaborate costumes. The unfurling of the giant silk Thangka is a once-in-a-lifetime sight. Gets crowded — arrive early morning.

Distance: 17km from Leh · Taxi ₹800–1,200 · Free entry
Lakes · Iconic

Pangong Tso Sunrise Camp

Spend the night at a lakeside camp at Pangong. Wake at 5am for a sunrise that shifts the lake through a dozen shades of blue, green, and silver in 45 minutes. This is what people mean when they say Ladakh changes you.

Camp cost: ₹1,500–3,000/night incl. meals · 3hr 30min from Leh

What to Pack

Clothing

  • Thermal base layers (even in July, nights are cold)
  • Down or fleece jacket — essential for mornings and passes
  • Waterproof outer shell jacket (August drizzles)
  • UV-protective trekking shirts × 4–5
  • Good trekking boots (waterproof, ankle support)
  • Comfortable sandals for town days
  • Neck gaiter / buff for high-altitude wind and sun
  • Warm hat and gloves (for Khardung La / Pangong nights)
  • UV-blocking sunglasses (critical at altitude)
  • SPF 50+ sunscreen × 2 bottles

Tech & Work

  • Laptop + charger (bring a multi-plug adaptor)
